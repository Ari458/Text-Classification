Tamil Nadu
May 17, 2015
Child Sexual Abuse, Rape
Description:
			A 41-year-old man from Tirupur district was sentenced to 10 years rigorous imprisonment by the district mahila court on Friday evening for raping three minor girls at Kulathupudur village near Mangalam in Tirupur in June 2013. The court has also slapped a fine of Rs 30,000. 

T Eswaramoorthy, from Mariamman Temple Street at Kulathupudur village in Tirupur district was working at a textile unit in Tirupur. His wife Jothi had passed away six years ago and he was living with his 11-year-old daughter, a Class 6 student. 

The three rape survivors were about nine years old and studied in class 4 at a government primary school in the same village. They were playing near a temple on June 15, 2013 and their parents had gone to work at textiles units. Eswaramoorthy was an alcoholic and did not go to work. He had approached the three minor girls and lured them to his house with chocolates. 

He raped the girls and threatened to kill them and their parents if they revealed anything to anyone. The girls told their parents who approached Palladam all-woman police station. Investigating officer S Kaleeswari registered a case against Eswaramoorthy under section 376 (2) f (commits rape on a woman when she is under 12 years of age) and section 3 and 4 of the protection of children from sexual offences (POCSO) Act, 2012. 

Tirupur mahila court special public prosecutor V Parimala sought life term for the accused. On Friday evening judge N Vasantha Leela sentenced him to thirty years of rigorous imprisonment (10 years RI for each girl's rape) and 21 years RI (each 7 years) under POCSO act. The court also slapped a fine of Rs 30,000 on him. The court said that the sentences would run concurrently and he would have to spend 10 years behind the bars.
