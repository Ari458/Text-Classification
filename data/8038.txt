Tamil Nadu
Oct 20, 2015
Abuse
Description:
			A sub-inspector and two constables were arrested by CBI in Chennai on Monday night for allegedly sexually assaulting a women in judicial custody. The accused were later produced before the chief judicial magistrate (CJM) in Coimbatore on Tuesday. 

The trio - SI K Vijayakumar, 33, constables S Thilak Kumar, 29 and S Ranganayagam, 31 - have been remanded to judicial custody till November 3 and lodged in Coimbatore central prison. 

In August 2014, the trio had taken a woman for questioning in connection with a murder case at Udumalpet. She was allegedly tortured and sexually harassed by the policemen at Udumalpet police station. Based on a petition from the daughter of the victim, Madras high court had ordered CBI inquiry into the case. 

CBI officials summoned all police personnel who were serving at Udumalpet police station when the alleged crime took place and arrested the trio after several rounds of questioning. 

They have been booked under sections 323 (voluntarily causing hurt), 325 (voluntarily causing grievous hurt), 331 (voluntarily causing hurt to extort confession or information or to compel restoration of property), 348 (wrongful confinement for the purpose of extorting property or constraining to an illegal act), 354 ( assault or used of criminal force to a woman with intent to outrage her modesty), 376 (2) (a) (b) (rape) r/w 34 (common intention) of the Indian Penal Code and under section 55 A of Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC).
