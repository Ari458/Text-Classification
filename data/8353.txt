Jharkhand
Jan 23, 2016
Molestation, Murder
Description:
			A fast-track court in the district sentenced a 28-year-old man to 10 years of rigorous imprisonment for attempting to molest and murder a 45-year-old widow 11 months ago.

Special judge Babita Prasad of the Bokaro fast-track court, besides awarding 10 years' RI to Santosh Thakur, also imposed a penalty of Rs 1,000 under Section 307 of the IPC, three years of rigorous imprisonment and Rs 500 fine for assaulting the victim with the intent to outrage her modesty under Section 354 of the IPC and one month imprisonment for wrongfully restraining the woman under Section 341.

"The punishment under all the three sections will run concurrently. The court has awarded the sentence related to the crime within 11 months of the incident," said Rakesh Rai, special public prosecutor.

The incident occurred on February 11, 2005, at Heertand under Balidih police station. The accused is a resident of Heertand. Rai said the woman was returning from work when Thakur attacked her. "The woman begged him to let her go and tried to resist his moves. This annoyed Thakur who then took out a knife and stabbed her three-four times till she collapsed ," said Rai.

The woman, who worked in a private firm, was later taken to Bokaro General Hospital. Police after lodging an FIR in this connection conducted raids and arrested Thakur, who was a vendor.
